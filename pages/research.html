Research

What is our purpose?

The PCGL offers a dynamic and supportive research environment spanning the full breadth of plant genomics. Master's students gain foundational experience in transcriptomics, while Doctoral students specialize in either digital breeding or genomics. PCGL also provides numerous opportunities in epigenomics, pan-genomics, population genomics, comparative genomics, phenomics, and AI applications in plant science.

We are deeply committed to nurturing the individual research goals of every lab member. Ultimately, PCGL aims to contribute to innovative crop breeding technologies addressing climate change and food security challenges.

Genome Assembly & Evolutionary Study

PCGL studies plant genomics using high-throughput sequencing technologies including NGS Illumina, Oxford Nanopore and PacBio. Our research focuses on constructing high-quality reference genomes for various plant species including crops and medicinal plants.

By analyzing genomic structures, genetic variations, and conserved regions, we investigate plant genome organization and evolutionary patterns.

Representative publications:
  • Jeon D, Sung YJ, Kim C*. High-quality Chromosomal-Level Genome Assembly of the Wasabi. Sci Data. 2024.
  • Choi S, Kang Y, Kim C*. Chromosome-level genome assembly of Salvia sclarea. Sci Data. 2025.
  • Lee S, Kim C*. Chromosome-scale genome assembly of Korean goosegrass. Sci Data. 2025.
  • Jeon D, Kim C*. Polyploids of Brassicaceae: Genomic Insights. Plants. 2024.

Digital Breeding

PCGL integrates large-scale genomic data with AI-driven predictive models to enhance breeding efficiency. By utilizing genotypic data, breeding cycles can be shortened, and selection accuracy for desirable traits can be improved compared to conventional breeding methods.

We apply statistical and AI-driven methods including regression analysis, machine learning (RF, SVM, RKHS), and deep learning models such as MLP, CNN, and RNN.

Representative publications:
  • Jeon D et al. Digitalizing breeding in plants. Front Plant Sci. 2023.
  • Kang Y et al. Optimizing genomic selection using K-wheat core collection. Front Plant Sci. 2023.
  • Jeon D et al. Genomic selection for tree morphological traits in Pinus koraiensis. Forestry. 2025.
